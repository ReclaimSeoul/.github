# Reclaim Seoul

**Combinatorial Material Reuse for a Temporal City**
AA Visiting School Seoul 2026 · Unit 3
Tutors: **Andrea Rossi + Hyo Wook Kim**

Reclaim Seoul is a digital repository, design archive, and toolkit developed for **Unit 3: Reclaimed Materials** at **AA Visiting School Seoul 2026**.

The unit investigates Seoul as a city of continuous transformation, where urban spaces, infrastructures, shops, streets, and temporary occupations change across the day, the week, and the seasons. Within this dynamic context, the workshop frames the city itself as a material resource: a field of reclaimed, residual, irregular, and readily available components that can be reactivated through computational design.

Rather than treating material irregularity, variability, and imperfection as problems, Reclaim Seoul uses them as generative drivers for discrete and combinatorial architectural systems. The work combines material scouting, fast digitization, computer-vision-assisted abstraction, Wasp aggregation logic, structural feedback, web-based cataloguing, and augmented-reality-guided assembly.

## Workflow

The Reclaim Seoul ecosystem is organized around a sequence of connected repositories and tools:

1. **Urban observation**
   Students collect material and urban mining observations across Seoul using field surveys and KoBoToolbox.

2. **Material documentation**
   Reclaimed components are documented as material passports in the **Reclaimed-Material-Bank**, including images, basic dimensions, material type, condition, source, and reuse notes.

3. **Design system development**
   Components are abstracted into reusable aggregation logics and stored in **Reclaimed-Design-Systems**.

4. **Design cataloguing**
   Final assemblies and spatial proposals are stored in **Reclaimed-Design-Catalog**, linking design metadata, geometry, thumbnails, and system references.

5. **Web visualization**
   Systems and designs are explored through the **Wasp Atlas Explorer** and the public **Reclaim Seoul** website.

6. **Augmented assembly**
   Selected assemblies can be prepared for AR-guided construction workflows, supporting part identification, assembly sequencing, and reversible construction logic.

## Workshop context

Reclaim Seoul was developed as part of **AAVS Seoul 2026: Emerged in Seoul**, organized through the **Social Algorithms Research Group**.

The broader programme investigated what has emerged from Seoul’s social, physical, local, and territorial realities. Unit 3 focused on **Reclaimed Materials**, exploring how residual and readily available urban materials can become the basis for adaptive, reversible, and time-based architectural systems.
